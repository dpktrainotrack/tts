About the Result Process

Usually, the GRE results can be checked online within 10-15 days after the test. Once the scores are ready, an email notification with detailed instructions on how to access them is sent to candidates. By using this email, students can access their ETS account via the official GRE portal in order to see their results. Moreover, from the same account, scores can be forwarded directly to the graduate schools, universities, or fellowship programs that the candidate chooses.

The GRE is scored on different scales: 130–170 for Verbal Reasoning, 130–170 for Quantitative Reasoning, and 0–6 for Analytical Writing. Besides the raw scores, ETS gives a percentile ranking, which makes it easier for the test takers to know their position relative to the others who took the GRE globally. This is very helpful when deciding which university to send your application to, as it allows you to know your strong and weak sides.

In order for it to be fair and trustworthy, ETS merges the score given by the AI system and the one given by a human expert. This system of double control ensures that the results are correct, authentic, and secure, and also that a mistake or a fraudulent activity does not happen.

Key Highlights of the GRE Result Process:

  • Results available online within 10–15 days.
  • Email notification sent with login instructions.
  • Scores are accessible through the ETS account portal.
  • Option to send results directly to institutions.
  • Scoring: 130–170 (Verbal & Quant), 0–6 (Writing).
  • Percentile ranks included for global comparison.
  • AI + human evaluation guarantees both justice and precision.

How to Prepare for Exam Day

  • Arrive Early: Reach the GRE test center at least 30 minutes before the reporting time to avoid last-minute stress or delays.
  • Have a Proper Meal: Do not skip breakfast. Eat a healthy meal to stay energized, and consider carrying a light snack or fruit for sustained energy.
  • Dress Comfortably: Wear loose and comfortable attire suitable for all temperatures, ensuring you remain relaxed throughout the exam.
  • Carry Valid Identification: Bring your passport as proof of identification. At the test centre, your photograph and biometrics will be captured and verified for official purposes.
