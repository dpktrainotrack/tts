Juniper Specialist certifications (JNCIS) are intermediate-level credentials that validate candidate’s expertise in Juniper’s technologies across various domains, including data centers, security, service provider networks, AI-driven networking, and enterprise routing & switching. These certifications are ideal for professionals who already have foundational networking knowledge and want to specialize in Juniper solutions for advanced roles.

The JNCIS exams are designed to test technical proficiency in specific areas such as configuration, troubleshooting, deployment, and operational practices. Candidates gain the ability to work with real-world Juniper technologies like Junos OS, firewalls, routing, switching, Mist AI-driven platforms, and advanced data center solutions. Each certification builds credibility in handling complex enterprise and service provider environments while aligning with industry best practices.

Juniper Specialist certifications include multiple tracks to match different career paths:

  • Data Center, Specialist (JNCIS-DC | JN0-683)
    Covers data center fabrics, EVPN-VXLAN, and Juniper’s data center solutions.
  • Security, Specialist (JNCIS-SEC | JN0-637 & JN0-336)
    Focuses on SRX Series devices, advanced firewall policies, VPNs, and security architecture.
  • Service Provider Routing & Switching, Specialist (JNCIS-SP | JN0-663)
    Concentrates on Junos-based routing, MPLS, BGP, and service provider-level technologies.
  • Mist AI Wired, Specialist (JNCIS-MistAI-Wired | JN0-750)
    Highlights AI-driven wired access solutions with Juniper’s Mist platform.
  • Mist AI Wireless, Specialist (JNCIS-MistAI-Wireless | JN0-460)
    Focuses on AI-driven wireless networking, WLAN troubleshooting, and optimization.
  • Enterprise Routing & Switching, Specialist (JNCIS-ENT | JN0-649)
    Validates advanced knowledge of Junos routing, switching, VLANs, and high-availability features.

By earning a JNCIS certification, candidates gain recognition as skilled professionals ready to design, implement, and manage Juniper-powered infrastructures in specialized domains. It also serves as a strong step toward advanced Juniper certifications like JNCIP (Professional) and JNCIE (Expert).
